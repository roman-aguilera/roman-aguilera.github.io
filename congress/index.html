Articles of Impleachment on Trump

Only one member of congress has called for articles of impeachment. Given the current horrors being comitted by ICE, this is an unnacteptable stance for our members of congress. Our representatives should not be afraid to stand up for their people, even if that means standing against this current administration. Our constition and laws are being violated on a daily basis, and we as a people should feel safe wherever we go.

Quality of Life Improvement

I beleive that policies should prioritize a meaningful increase in quality of life first, not revenue or economic activity for the sake of economic activity. This includes affordable and secure housing, upwards mobility, educational opportunity, prductivity through automation, and peace at a local and international level.

Education Reform

I beleive there needs to be major education reform. First, North County has need for a new university to be added that is meant to serve Santa Maria, Guadalupe, Lompoc, and the surrounding areas. This would drive up the possibilities for economic, cultural, educational, and technical exchange in manner that is more equtable. The United States is widely called the land of opportunity, and I beleive this would increase opportunity where there is lack of it. Secondly, existing University need to be more equtable in their administration. Secondly I would push to make Universities solely on the semester system. The quarter systems quick administrative turnarouns increase stress for students and staff, simply for the benefit of more revenue, rather than allowing for a quality of education.

ABOUT

Roman Aguilera is a robotics researcher and engineer with expertise in AI-driven robotic learning, control systems, and reinforcement learning. He was previosly an AI and Machine Learning Engineering Lead at PivotalVC. Prior to that, he was a PhD student for 6 years in Computer Science, wokring on the intersetion of Robotics and AI at the University of California, Santa Barbara. He holds a Master of Science in Computer Science from the University of California, Santa Barbara, and a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering from the University of California, San Diego. His work includes research in robotic arm control, agricultural automation, and wearable health monitoring, with published research in Advanced Materials.
