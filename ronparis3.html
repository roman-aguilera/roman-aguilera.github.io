Ron Paris

About Me

Ron Paris is an American R&B and soul musician known for his work as a singer, performer, and educator. His music blends classic R&B, doo-wop, gospel, and soul, often featuring deeply personal and socially aware lyrics.

Musical Career

  • Paris has been active as a performer for decades, building a reputation rooted in authentic, retro-inspired soul sounds. (The Channels)
  • He released albums including Soul Mate, featuring classic soul influences, original compositions, smooth vocals, and heartfelt songwriting.
  • His work includes reinterpretations of soul classics, such as a modernized version of Al Green’s Let’s Stay Together.

Performance & Influence

  • Paris has performed at multicultural festivals and events, using music as a tool for education and cultural reflection. (Santa Barbara Independent)
  • He was once part of Buck Ram’s The Platters and opened for The Jacksons before they became The Jackson 5.
  • Paris had his own show in Las Vegas before relocating to Santa Barbara, where he continued his artistic and community work.

Legacy & Approach

Ron Paris uses his voice not only to entertain, but also to educate audiences about the history and cultural significance of soul and R&B, reaching both longtime fans and younger generations.
